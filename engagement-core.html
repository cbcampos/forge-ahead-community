Engaging Communities for Health Equity

The Community Engagement Core of the Forge AHEAD Center seeks to promote health equity and reduce the burden of cardiometabolic diseases across the Deep South by developing engaged, active, and sustained partnerships with diverse regional stakeholders.

We believe that meaningful change happens when communities are true partners in research—not just participants, but collaborators who help shape priorities, design programs, and share findings in ways that resonate with local culture and needs.

"We believe that the best solutions come from within the communities themselves."

— Caroline Compretta, PhD, Community Engagement Core Lead

Our Goal

Facilitating bi-directional communication and authentic partnerships

Facilitate bi-directional communication and authentic partnerships between Center members and diverse regional stakeholders and disseminate relevant and actionable strategies to address the persistent chronic disease burden.

Specific Aims

Our core objectives for community engagement across the region

🤝

Aim 1

Expand Partnerships

Expand and maintain partnerships with diverse non-academic partners including community organizations, faith-based institutions, healthcare providers, and government agencies across Alabama, Mississippi, and Louisiana.

💬

Aim 2

Facilitate Co-Learning

Facilitate co-learning and bi-directional communication between the Center's Research and Pilot Project investigators and community stakeholders, ensuring research addresses real community needs.

📢

Aim 3

Disseminate Findings

Develop and disseminate findings from the Center to diverse audiences including community residents, policymakers, educators, public health practitioners, and academic researchers.

How We Work

Our approach to authentic community partnership

Bi-Directional Communication

We believe research should flow in both directions. Our community partners help identify priorities and inform research design, while we share findings in accessible, culturally appropriate ways.

  • Regular community listening sessions
  • Research priority setting with community input
  • Plain-language research summaries
  • Community-friendly educational materials

Sustained Partnerships

We move beyond one-time interactions to build lasting relationships that create real impact. Our partners are involved at every stage of research, from conception to dissemination.

  • Long-term collaborative relationships
  • Shared decision-making processes
  • Joint planning and implementation
  • Mutual capacity building
