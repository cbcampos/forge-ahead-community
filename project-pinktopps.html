Pinktopps

A survivorship program helping young breast cancer survivors build long-term wellness through peer support, practical tools, and whole-person care.

All recipients
Overview / Projects / Pinktopps
Program Focus

Helping survivors move from treatment into healthier daily life.

Pinktopps supports young breast cancer survivors, especially women ages 19 to 45 and those with limited access to healthcare follow-up, through peer-led support, wellness education, mental health support, and practical community connections.

Wellness Wheel

A whole-person approach to survivorship.

Physical wellnessNutritionEmotional wellnessSocial support
What the Program Covers
  • Movement, rest, and physical recovery strategies
  • Meal planning and practical nutrition support
  • Peer circles, counseling, and journaling
  • Mindfulness, relationship support, and community reconnection
Why It Matters

Life after treatment often comes with emotional, physical, and practical challenges that do not end when appointments become less frequent. Pinktopps helps survivors build sustainable routines, trusted support, and a stronger sense of control over long-term wellness.
