Who Joins The Coalition

The coalition reflects the full breadth of community-engaged collaboration.

Community-based organizations

Organizations rooted in advocacy, neighborhood support, food access, education, and local wellness work.

Faith, civic, and resident leaders

Trusted voices who help connect Forge AHEAD to local priorities, communication channels, and lived experience.

Public health and healthcare partners

Health systems, clinics, health departments, and public agencies supporting outreach, implementation, and referral pathways.

Academic partners

University and technical partners who work with communities through co-learning, dissemination, and shared problem-solving.

What Coalition Membership Supports

Joining the coalition means joining a regional network focused on health disparities, partnership, and translational impact.

1

Connect across the region

Meet community and academic partners who are working to improve cardiometabolic health in the Deep South.

2

Explore partnership opportunities

3

How The Coalition Works

Coalition members help strengthen community engagement across the center.

Relationship building

The coalition helps develop new partnerships with diverse non-academic organizations and strengthens existing regional ties.

Bi-directional communication

Coalition meetings create opportunities for focused, bidirectional communication between investigators, partners, and community stakeholders.

Community dissemination

The coalition helps move findings, resources, and opportunities outward to residents, educators, practitioners, and policymakers.

Coalition Priorities
  • Build a stronger network of academic and non-academic partners across the region.
  • Keep community priorities visible in communication, engagement, and dissemination planning.
  • Create opportunities for collaboration across organizations with shared interests.
  • Support strategies that respond to the ongoing burden of chronic disease in the Deep South.
SHAPES

SHAPES supports focused, in-depth discussion about projects and partnerships that need follow-up.

In addition to regularly scheduled Community Coalition meetings, community stakeholders and investigators may take part in StakeHolder Advisory PanEls, or SHAPES. SHAPES is designed to encourage focused, in-depth, bidirectional discourse around potential projects and partnerships identified for follow-up during coalition meetings.
