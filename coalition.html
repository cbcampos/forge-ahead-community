Community Coalition

Bringing together diverse partners to address regional health disparities

Community › Community Coalition

Uniting for Health Equity

The Community Coalition brings together non-academic and academic partners to explore and develop innovative partnerships that address regional health disparities and further the translational research goals of the Forge AHEAD Center.

By connecting organizations across sectors and states, we create a powerful network dedicated to improving cardiometabolic health outcomes throughout the Deep South.

"Working together, we can achieve more than any single organization could alone. The Community Coalition amplifies our collective impact on health equity."

— Forge AHEAD Community Engagement Team

SHAPES Program

StakeHolder Advisory PanEls for Focused Collaboration

What is SHAPES?

In addition to potential interactions during regularly scheduled Community Coalition meetings, community stakeholders or investigators may partake in StakeHolder Advisory PanEls (SHAPES).

The purpose of SHAPES is to encourage focused, in-depth, bidirectional discourse regarding potential projects and partnerships that are identified for follow-up during regularly scheduled Coalition meetings.

💡

Key Benefits

  • Deep dive into specific topics
  • Direct input on research design
  • Partnership development
  • Actionable outcomes

Membership Details

Coalition members represent organizations and potential organizational resources, working together to advance health equity across the Deep South.

Meeting Schedule

  • Community Coalition meetings are scheduled on an ad hoc basis
  • SHAPES meetings are arranged as specific needs arise
  • Virtual attendance options available

Member Benefits

  • Access to Forge AHEAD research findings
  • Networking opportunities with partner organizations
  • Potential for collaborative research programs
  • Input on center priorities and initiatives

Member Expectations

Coalition members are expected to:

  • Attend scheduled meetings when possible
  • Share relevant organizational resources and expertise
  • Participate in SHAPES meetings when invited
  • Help identify community needs and priorities
  • Support dissemination of research findings
  • Advocate for health equity in their communities
