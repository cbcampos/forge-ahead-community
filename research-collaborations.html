Community-Engaged Research

Research partnerships work best when collaboration, feedback, and shared benefit continue throughout the project.

Forge AHEAD treats community engagement as an ongoing relationship between researchers and communities. Community input continues to shape the work as projects move forward.

1

Describe the project in plain language

Summarize who the project is for, what participation looks like, and why the work matters to the communities involved.

2

Share materials for review

Bring recruitment language, flyers, handouts, or talking points that community members may see.

3

Receive CAB guidance

Use review feedback to improve clarity, accessibility, burden, relevance, and trust.

4

Prepare for launch

Finalize materials and coordination plans before public rollout or participant recruitment.

Before You Submit

Share the information the CAB needs for a meaningful review.

  • Project overview and purpose
  • Who the project is intended to support
  • Time commitment and participation details
  • Community-facing materials for review
  • Partner contacts and desired timeline
What to Expect

What to expect from CAB review

CAB review helps strengthen how a project is described, how participation is explained, and how well the work reflects community priorities and practical realities. The goal is to improve materials, outreach, and implementation plans before moving forward.

Community

Brings lived experience, local knowledge, and practical insight about what will or will not work.

Collaboration

Creates shared understanding around goals, design, language, outreach, and implementation.

Feedback & Benefits

Improves materials and methods while helping findings, tools, and support flow back into the community.

Long-Term Partnerships

Builds trust over time so relationships continue beyond a single study or grant period.

What CAB Review Strengthens
  • Clarity of recruitment and outreach language
  • Fit with community priorities and local context
  • Accessibility of materials and participation steps
  • Participant burden, trust, and communication flow
Community-facing standard

Projects should be understandable to the people they are meant to serve.

That is part of Forge AHEAD’s broader partnership model: collaboration should be understandable, feedback should matter, and communities should experience real benefit from the relationship.
