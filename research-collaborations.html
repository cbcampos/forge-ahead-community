Research Collaborations

A simpler, community-facing explanation of how project review works.

The expanded planning documents called for a dedicated collaboration page. This page explains the CAB review concept in plain language and creates a destination for future submission forms.

Overview / Research Collaborations
1

Describe the project in plain language

Summarize who the project is for, what participation looks like, and why the work matters to the communities involved.

2

Share materials for review

Bring recruitment language, flyers, handouts, or talking points that community members may see.

3

Receive CAB guidance

Use review feedback to improve clarity, accessibility, burden, relevance, and trust.

4

Prepare for launch

Finalize materials and coordination plans before public rollout or participant recruitment.

Submission Checklist

What this page can ask for later

  • Project overview and purpose
  • Who the project is intended to support
  • Time commitment and participation details
  • Community-facing materials for review
  • Partner contacts and desired timeline
Built for Expansion

Ready for uploads, notifications, and review criteria

Once final process owners and timelines are confirmed, this page can support a real submission form, file upload field, rubric summary, and automatic routing to staff or CAB contacts.

What CAB review can improve
  • Clarity of recruitment and outreach language
  • Fit with community priorities and local context
  • Accessibility of materials and participation steps
  • Participant burden, trust, and communication flow
Community-facing standard

People should be able to understand a project without reading like an IRB reviewer.

This page is meant to make that expectation visible to collaborators: plain language, transparent participation details, and clear respect for community time and knowledge.
