Edwards Street Fellowship Center: Food Is Medicine

A Hattiesburg program connecting patients with diabetes, obesity, and hypertension to fresh produce, nutrition education, and regular health monitoring.

Visit Edwards StreetAll recipients
Overview / Projects / Edwards Street Fellowship Center
Program Focus

Fresh produce, practical education, and clinical follow-up working together.

Edwards Street Fellowship Center’s project provides fresh produce vouchers to clinic patients while pairing that support with chronic condition education, nutrition consultations, and regular tracking of blood pressure, A1C, and weight over time.

Who It Serves

Low-income patients of the Fellowship Health Clinic who need practical support managing chronic conditions.

Food accessDiabetes careHypertensionFree clinic support
What Participants Receive
  • Produce vouchers for fresh fruits and vegetables at a local grocery store
  • One-on-one chronic condition education with nurse educators
  • Nutrition consultations and practical portion tools
  • Ongoing tracking of A1C, blood pressure, and weight
Why It Matters

Managing diabetes and hypertension is much harder when fresh food is out of reach. This project connects nutrition, education, and consistent clinical follow-up so patients can build healthier routines with concrete support.

The work also shows how local clinics, volunteers, and university partners can collaborate around practical barriers that shape everyday health.
