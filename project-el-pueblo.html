El Pueblo: Mi Corazon

A bilingual heart-health program supporting Hispanic adults with limited English proficiency as they manage blood pressure, build healthy habits, and navigate care with confidence.

Visit El Pueblo All recipients
Overview / Projects / El Pueblo
Program Focus

Heart health support built around language access, trust, and steady follow-up.

Mi Corazon helps Hispanic adults with limited English proficiency manage high blood pressure through weekly blood pressure checks, bilingual check-ins, goal setting, and monthly in-person education sessions focused on nutrition, physical activity, stress management, and practical self-care.

Who It Serves

Hispanic adults in Mississippi who face language barriers and limited access to affordable care.

Bilingual supportHypertensionCommunity healthTrusted outreach
How the Program Works
  • Spanish-language outreach through clinics, schools, churches, and community centers
  • Weekly blood pressure checks with in-person, phone, or virtual follow-up
  • Monthly education sessions with healthy meals and time for questions
  • Support with medical appointments, medication reminders, and health navigation
Early Impact

Participants are reporting stronger follow-up with primary care, lower-sodium eating habits, regular walking routines, and consistent engagement with monthly meetings, even when transportation is difficult.

The program aims to serve at least 20 participants directly while also reaching families and the wider community through shared sessions and trusted local relationships.
