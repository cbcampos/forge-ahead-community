Community Micro-Grant Recipient

MetroMorphosis

A resident-led community garden project in Baton Rouge that improves access to fresh food, reduces neighborhood blight, and builds local leadership.

Visit MetroMorphosisAll recipients
Overview / Projects / MetroMorphosis
Program Focus

Transforming an empty lot into a community asset for food, learning, and connection.

The Uptown Community Garden in South Baton Rouge converts a blighted lot into a sustainable growing space with resident input, healthy produce, local workshops, and neighborhood stewardship.

What Happens There

Fresh food, shared learning, and resident leadership in one neighborhood-based space.

Community gardenFood accessUrban farmingResident leadership
Project Activities
  • Raised garden beds and irrigation systems
  • Quarterly workshops on urban farming, composting, gardening, and healthy cooking
  • Garden Steward training for neighborhood residents
  • Produce distributions and community meals with priority for elders and families facing transportation barriers
Why It Matters

The garden addresses more than beautification. It responds to limited access to fresh food, neighborhood blight, and the need for places where neighbors can learn, gather, and lead together.

MetroMorphosis is also exploring aquaponics as a next step in sustainable food production and resident education.
