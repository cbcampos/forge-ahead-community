Community Micro-Grants

Supporting local organizations that are already doing trusted, community-rooted work.

The Community Micro-Grant Program supports organizations leading practical, community-rooted health work across the Deep South and highlights the local groups currently featured on the site.

Overview / Community Micro-Grants
Purpose

Invest in local solutions

Micro-grants help organizations test practical ideas that reflect local needs, culture, and trusted community relationships.

Support

Pair funding with technical assistance

Recipients benefit from check-ins, shared learning, and support that helps organizations move from idea to implementation.

Visibility

Share what communities are building

Recipient spotlights help communities see how local ideas become practical, visible action in neighborhoods, clinics, and community settings.

Funding Priorities

Examples of the kinds of work supported through the program

  • Community-led education and prevention efforts
  • Programs that reduce barriers to healthy living
  • Initiatives grounded in trust, culture, and local knowledge
  • Projects that can share practical lessons back to the region
Current Recipients

Five organizations are currently featured.

The current recipient spotlights feature El Pueblo, Edwards Street Fellowship Center, BirthWell Partners, Pinktopps, and MetroMorphosis. Each page explains the organization’s work in plain language and shows how the project responds to a local health need.

"The structure gave us a way to move an idea into a community-facing program with real support behind it."
Recipient perspective
"A micro-grant can be small in dollars and still big in community trust, visibility, and momentum."
Program insight
Application Journey

A clearer process for organizations exploring the program.

1

Review fit

Understand priorities, eligibility, and the kinds of local initiatives the micro-grant program supports.

2

Prepare a short concept

Describe the need, who benefits, and how the effort connects to practical community health outcomes.

3

Receive support

Use meetings and technical assistance to refine implementation plans, reporting expectations, and learning opportunities.
