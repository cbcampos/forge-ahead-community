Community Micro-Grant Program

Empowering local organizations to drive health improvements in their communities

Community › Community Micro-Grant Program

Empowering Communities with Targeted Support

The Deep South faces some of the highest rates of chronic disease in the country, and many local organizations are already leading efforts to improve community health.

The Forge AHEAD Community Micro-Grant Program is designed to provide the financial resources and support these groups need to scale their efforts and drive lasting change.

"This micro-grant program allows us to directly support organizations that are already working to improve the lives of their neighbors. By providing funding, resources, and ongoing support, we can help amplify their impact and create lasting change."

— Caroline Compretta, PhD, Community Engagement Core Lead

Funding Priorities

What we look for in micro-grant applications

📖

Health Education

Projects that increase knowledge about diabetes, obesity, hypertension, and related conditions.

🛡️

Disease Prevention

Initiatives focused on preventing chronic disease before it starts through lifestyle interventions.

🏥

Access to Care

Programs that connect underserved communities with healthcare services and resources.

How Projects Are Evaluated

Criteria used to select funded projects

Key Criteria

  • Feasibility — Can the project be successfully implemented with available resources?
  • Innovation — Does the project bring fresh approaches to community health?
  • Collaboration — Does the project involve partnerships with other organizations?
  • Sustainability — Can the project continue after the funding period ends?

Impact Focus

  • Community Need — Does the project address a demonstrated community need?
  • Reach — How many people will benefit from the project?
  • Measurable Outcomes — Are there clear goals and ways to track progress?
  • Cultural Relevance — Does the project reflect the community it serves?

Support for Grantees

We're invested in your success

📅

Monthly Meetings

Regular check-ins with the Forge AHEAD team to discuss progress, share challenges, and celebrate successes.

📊

Mid-Year Review

A progress check-in at the halfway point to ensure projects are on track and make any needed adjustments.

📝

Impact Report

Guidance on documenting your project's outcomes and sharing your success story with the community.

Community-Led Guiding

One of the unique aspects of the micro-grant program is the involvement of the Forge AHEAD Community Advisory Board (CAB), which plays a critical role in guiding the center's initiatives and ensuring that its work is responsive to the real needs of the communities it serves.

CAB members help review applications, provide feedback on project design, and ensure that funded projects align with community priorities.

"Our communities have the knowledge, passion, and solutions to tackle health challenges—we just need the resources to make it happen. This grant program is an incredible opportunity for local organizations to access funding and support that will help turn their ideas into action."

— CAB Member

Eligibility

Who can apply for a micro-grant

Eligible Organizations

  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • Faith-based organizations with community programs
  • Community health organizations
  • Local government agencies
  • Tribal organizations
  • Schools and educational institutions

Geographic Focus

Projects must serve communities in one or more of our three-state partnership area:

  • Alabama
  • Mississippi
  • Louisiana
