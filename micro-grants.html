Community Micro-Grants

Supporting local organizations that are already doing trusted, community-rooted work.

This page shifts the original grant summary toward a clearer community-facing program page with funding priorities, support expectations, and room for awardee spotlights.

Overview / Community Micro-Grants
Purpose

Invest in local solutions

Micro-grants can help organizations test practical ideas that reflect local needs, culture, and trusted community relationships.

Support

Pair funding with technical assistance

The planning materials point to check-ins, peer learning, and structure that helps organizations move from idea to implementation.

Visibility

Share what communities are building

The site can spotlight awardees, lessons learned, and downloadable impact snapshots once launch assets are approved.

Funding Priorities

Examples of work this page is prepared to highlight

  • Community-led education and prevention efforts
  • Programs that reduce barriers to healthy living
  • Initiatives grounded in trust, culture, and local knowledge
  • Projects that can share practical lessons back to the region

Application Journey

A clearer process for organizations exploring the program.

1

Review fit

Understand priorities, eligibility, and what kinds of local initiatives the micro-grant program is designed to support.

2

Prepare a short concept

Describe the need, who benefits, and how the effort connects to practical community health outcomes.

3

Receive support

Use meetings and technical assistance to refine implementation plans, reporting expectations, and learning opportunities.
